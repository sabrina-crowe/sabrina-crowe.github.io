Education

I'm a freshman at Stevens Institute of Technology. I chose Stevens because of its unique Undergraduate Quantitative Finance Program; I initially applied to schools planning on double majoring in Applied Mathematics and Finance, but I then discovered QF included both subjects as well as a vital Computer Science & Data Science component. The program, alongside the location and scholarships, made Stevens a no-brainer.

In high school I graduated top 5 in the class through our school's STEM Academy; I followed the mathematics track and had the privilege of taking Linear Algebra as a senior, my favorite math subject. I was involved with the high school's varsity Womens swim team, the Kiwanis Key Club, and the Math Team. I also tutored my peers through the Math Honor Society and National Honor Society.

At Stevens, I'm a part of the Pinnacle Scholars program, the Web Chair of the Python for Business Club, and a member of Stevens Women in Business and Computer Science. I'm excited to discover other ways I can become involved in the Stevens community.

Last Trading Day Price Downloader

I created a program which downloads the prices of desired stocks for the last trading day of each given year. It outputs the data as a .csv file. This program's intended use is for calculating CAGR in Excel. Tools include:

  • Python (Pandas & yfinance)
  • Tkinter (GUI Package)
  • cx_Freeze (.exe compiler)

    Data Analysis: Type II Diabetes in Women

    This was my first project involving data analysis. I used data from Kaggle that recorded the potential risk factors for Type II Diabetes in women. I then used the data to train a logistic regression model to predict whether a woman would be diagnosed with Diabetes. I had to clean the data, as some of it was invalid or implausible. Tools include:

  • Python (Pandas)
  • Data Visualization (Matplotlib, Seaborn)
  • Logistic Regression (sklearn)
